Chicken Biryani

A fragrant, flavorful rice dish made with tender chicken, aromatic spices, and basmati rice — slow-cooked to perfection for a rich, traditional Indian taste.

Ingredients

  • For Chicken Marinade:
    • Chicken - 1 kg (bone-in preferred)
    • Thick curd (yogurt) - 1 cup
    • Ginger-garlic paste - 2 tbsp
    • Green chilies - 4 (slit)
    • Red chili powder - 1½ tsp
    • Turmeric powder - ½ tsp
    • Garam masala - 1 tsp
    • Lemon juice - 2 tbsp
    • Fried onions (birista) - 1 cup
    • Fresh coriander leaves - ½ cup (chopped)
    • Mint leaves - ½ cup (chopped)
    • Salt - to taste
    • Oil - 3 tbsp
  • For Rice:
    • Basmati rice - 2 cups (soaked 30 min)
    • Whole spices - 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon, 1 star anise
    • Salt - to taste
  • For Dum (Layering):
    • Saffron - few strands soaked in 3 tbsp warm milk
    • Fried onions - handful
    • Mint and coriander leaves - handful
    • Ghee - 2 tbsp

Instructions

  1. Marinate Chicken
    • In a large bowl, mix all marinade ingredients.
    • Add chicken and coat well.
    • Cover and let it rest for at least 2-4 hours (overnight gives best flavor).
  2. Parboil Rice
    • Boil 6-7 cups water with salt and whole spices.
    • Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (grain should be slightly firm).
    • Drain and keep aside.
  3. Layering Biryani
    • In a heavy-bottomed pot or biryani handi, spread marinated chicken evenly at the bottom.
    • Layer half the rice on top.
    • Sprinkle mint, coriander, fried onions, saffron milk, and 1 tbsp ghee.
    • Add remaining rice and repeat toppings.
    • Drizzle the rest of ghee around the edges.
  4. Dum Cooking
    • Option 1 – Stovetop: Seal lid with wheat dough or cover tightly. Cook on high heat for 10 minutes, then low flame for 25–30 minutes. Rest for 10 minutes before opening.
    • Option 2 – Oven: Bake covered at 180°C (350°F) for 30–35 minutes.
  5. Serve
    • Gently mix from the bottom before serving.
    • Serve hot with raita, salad, and boiled eggs if you like.
